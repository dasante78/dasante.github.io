About

I’m focused on building impactful technologies and applying AI in meaningful ways to solve real-world problems.

I am currently an AI engineer at Distyl AI. Previously, I completed my BS at MIT in Artificial Intelligence and Decision-Making, followed by a Master of Engineering in the Computational Cardiovascular Research Group, where I was advised by Collin Stultz.

Papers

Position: Evaluation of ECG Representations Must Be Fixed

ICML 2026 position track

Daniel Prakah-Asante, Zachary Berger, John Guttag, and Collin M. Stultz. A position paper arguing that ECG representation benchmarks should better reflect clinically meaningful objectives, including structural disease, patient-level forecasting, and standardized evaluation practices for imbalanced multi-label settings.

Forget, Forget Me Not: Adaptive Token Retention for LLMs

Explores adaptive token retention methods for reducing LLM sequence length and compute while preserving key information. The project compares random, MLP, CNN-MLP, RNN, and identity retention masks with reinforcement-learning based training and evaluation workflows.
